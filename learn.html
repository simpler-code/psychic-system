Learn Chess

Master the royal game with our comprehensive guide — from basic rules to winning strategies.

Chess Rules

Chess is a two-player strategy game played on an 8x8 checkered board. Each player begins with 16 pieces: one King, one Queen, two Rooks, two Bishops, two Knights, and eight Pawns. The objective is to checkmate your opponent's King.

Piece Movements

Piece Movement Value
♔ King One square in any direction Infinite (the game)
♕ Queen Any number of squares diagonally, horizontally, or vertically 9 points
♖ Rook Any number of squares horizontally or vertically 5 points
♗ Bishop Any number of squares diagonally 3 points
♘ Knight L-shape: 2 squares in one direction, then 1 square perpendicular 3 points
♙ Pawn Forward 1 square (2 from starting position), captures diagonally 1 point

Special Rules

Castling is a special move involving the King and a Rook. The King moves two squares toward a Rook, and the Rook jumps to the square the King crossed. Conditions: neither piece has moved, the path is clear, and the King is not in check nor passes through check.

En Passant allows a pawn that has just moved two squares forward to be captured by an opposing pawn as if it had moved only one square. This capture must be performed immediately after the two-square advance.

Promotion occurs when a pawn reaches the opposite end of the board. It must be exchanged for a Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight of the same color (almost always a Queen).

Chess Strategy

Opening Principles

  • Control the center — Occupy or influence the central squares (e4, d4, e5, d5) with pawns and pieces.
  • Develop your pieces — Bring your Knights and Bishops into active play early. Avoid moving the same piece multiple times in the opening.
  • Castle early — Protect your King by castling, ideally within the first 10 moves.
  • Don't bring the Queen out too early — She can be harassed by lesser pieces and waste tempi.

Middle Game Strategy

  • Create a plan — Identify weaknesses in your opponent's position and formulate a plan to exploit them.
  • Coordinate your pieces — Pieces work best together. Rooks belong on open files, bishops on long diagonals.
  • Look for tactics — Always be alert for forks, pins, skewers, discovered attacks, and deflection.
  • Manage pawn structure — Avoid doubled, isolated, and backward pawns which become targets.

Endgame Principles

  • Activate your King — In the endgame, the King becomes a powerful fighting piece.
  • Promote a pawn — Creating a passed pawn and promoting it is often the path to victory.
  • Reduce to won positions — Simplify by trading pieces when you have a material advantage.
  • Key squares — Learn key squares and opposition in King and pawn endings.

Tips for Improvement

Solve puzzles daily — Even 15 minutes of tactical training will dramatically improve your game. Visit our Chess Puzzles page to practice.

Analyze your games — After each game, review your moves and identify mistakes. Learning from your own errors is the fastest path to improvement.

Study master games — Reviewing games by grandmasters helps you understand strategic concepts and typical patterns.

Practice with our AI — Play regularly against our chess engine to apply what you've learned in real games.
