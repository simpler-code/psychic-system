Opening Explorer

Study the most popular chess openings, their move orders, and strategic ideas to improve your game from move one.

Open Games (1.e4 e5)

Italian Game

C50

A classic opening focusing on rapid development and central control. White aims for d4 to dominate the center.

1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4

Ruy Lopez (Spanish Opening)

C60

One of the oldest and most respected openings. White puts immediate pressure on the knight defending e5.

1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bb5

Scotch Game

C44

An aggressive opening where White opens the center early, leading to tactical complications.

1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.d4

Vienna Game

C25

White develops the kingside knight early, keeping options for f4 or a quiet d3 setup.

1.e4 e5 2.Nc3

King's Gambit

C30

An aggressive gambit where White sacrifices a pawn for rapid development and attacking chances against f7.

1.e4 e5 2.f4

Evans Gambit

C51

A bold gambit offering a pawn to gain time and control of the center with rapid piece development.

1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4 Bc5 4.b4

Semi-Open Games (1.e4, Black plays differently)

Sicilian Defense

B20

The most popular response to 1.e4. Black fights for the d5 square and creates an asymmetrical, dynamic position.

1.e4 c5

French Defense

C00

A solid defense where Black challenges the e4 pawn and aims for a strong pawn chain with d5 and e6.

1.e4 e6

Caro-Kann Defense

B10

A reliable and solid opening. Black prepares d5 on firm foundations without blocking the c8-bishop.

1.e4 c6

Pirc Defense

B07

A hypermodern defense where Black allows White to build a large center before counterattacking it.

1.e4 d6 2.d4 Nf6

Alekhine Defense

B02

A provocative opening where Black invites White's pawns forward, planning to undermine them later.

1.e4 Nf6

Modern Defense

B06

Black fianchettoes the kingside bishop and adopts a flexible, hypermodern setup against any White system.

1.e4 g6

Closed Games (1.d4 or other)

Queen's Gambit

D06

One of the most played openings at all levels. White offers a temporary pawn sacrifice to control the center.

1.d4 d5 2.c4

King's Indian Defense

E60

A counterattacking system where Black allows White to build a pawn center, then strikes back with e5 or c5.

1.d4 Nf6 2.c4 g6

Nimzo-Indian Defense

E20

Black pins the knight on c3, exerting pressure on White's center and creating doubled pawns.

1.d4 Nf6 2.c4 e6 3.Nc3 Bb4

Slav Defense

D10

A solid response to the Queen's Gambit where Black supports d5 with c6 while keeping the c8-bishop active.

1.d4 d5 2.c4 c6

Catalan Opening

E00

White combines the Queen's Gambit with a fianchetto of the kingside bishop, creating long-term pressure.

1.d4 Nf6 2.c4 e6 3.g3

London System

D02

A solid, easy-to-learn system where White develops the bishop to f4 and builds a sturdy position.

1.d4 d5 2.Bf4
